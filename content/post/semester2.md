Followed by semester 1 and the holidays in which I tried to learn some web development with Django. My experience with Django was not that good. As a beginner, I searched over the internet for the framework to start web development. After having a glimpse over the documentation of both the frameworks, I found Flask to be very straightforward. There was no magic, just a simple API to get. Further, while I intended to implement an [MVC](https://www.w3schools.in/mvc-architecture/) architecture and found both the frameworks were very much supportive.

The reason why I chose Django over Flask was because of the community impact. Django's community is very strong, particularly on IRC. [/r/Django](https://reddit.com/r/django/) is far more active than [/r/flask](https://reddit.com/r/flask/).

However, the problems that I faced with Django as a beginner was that with Django, I often found myself having to learn why things were structured the way there were before I could learn the system. An example of this would be forms processing - on Django, I remember having to learn about the Forms models and all of their configuration. Though I was able to make a simple polling app using Django, I was never able to understand the basics of web development such as REST architecture, Database Management, CRUD etc. which I learnt with time by analyzing open-source projects.

Apart from that beginning of Semester 2 was unexpected. I became a part of team NARCODES. We wrote 3 proposals [SIH-19](https://pnijhara.me/2020/01/journey-to-sih-19/) and got selected for the final round. It was the first time I worked on Flask which made me realize why choosing Django was not the good idea as a beginner [^bignote]. There I developed a library of helpers functions and followed architectural conventions, and thus have learned a lot about Python in the process.

Tried my luck with GSOC but couldn’t make it up to there. Wrote 2 proposals for [FOSSASIA](https://github.com/fossasia/) and [CBMI@UTHSC](https://github.com/cbmi-uthsc/). Apart from all the coding stuff my hockey practice also went with pace and thus got the chance to play at the inter hall hockey tournament. Got selected as the computer team [^2] member for the [AUV club of the college](https://auvzhcet.in). Well again, the curriculum of AMU is easy from other universities so I was able to score decent marks.

Final Exams got over, time for vacations and new plans. I decided to learn the concepts of ROS. And wait for the beginning of next semester……………..

[^bignote]: I think I'll ultimately end up using Django for prototyping and rapid application development. But I stand behind my contention that Flask is better suited to a new Python programmer.
