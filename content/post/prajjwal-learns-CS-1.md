More than Computer Science itself, I am beginning a new phase of my journey, where now I will not write much code. However, it is all intended towards the theoretical aspect of the things I used to love. I am committing 5 to 6 hours a day on weekdays and a tiny to the blogs. The rest of the time will go to my major degree (Electrical Engineering).

Many asked me why I am learning CS along with Electrical. Some of the questions were very very strange. They were "if you don't like electrical engineering then why you opt for it?" or "maybe you are scoring less in electricals that's why you are opting for something easy". The answer to these type of comments/questions is very simple. I am not moving away from electrical engineering. I will learn both fields simultaneously. And not to brag, I have a reasonable amount of knowledge of Electrical with decent marks.

The idea of learning CS is to make my scope broader. The idea of bringing automation to electrical engineering is driving me to learn CS. Maybe in future, I will be integrating some of the theoretical CS with EE.

Moving back to the topic, I have arranged all possible resources for learning CS. Most of them are free and available on the internet. Started CS with operating system. 

What did I do/learn today?
1. How are computer work
2. Hardware and software integration
3. Types of computer software
4. What is OS and types of OS in detail

And that’s about it.
Looking back at it, I wonder why this took me three long hours, but it is what it is. I’d rather show up daily and make progress.
