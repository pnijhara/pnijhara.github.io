Aligarh Muslim University, the university I chose for pursuing my bachelors. It was not an easy decision, but somehow I managed to get in there. Electrical Engineering was never in my list, but when are unable to get your dream branch you have to go with what's written in faith and so, I am now studying Electrical Engineering.

A first month away from home was like jumping straight into hell. I never saw that much amount of new people all of a sudden. I managed to make myself comfortable away from home, kept myself busy with college stuff. One of the best platforms I encountered was the AMU-OSS (Open source software group of Aligarh Muslim University). There I met a lot of awsome seniors who never saw me as there junior rather they treated me as there brother. Though I was familiar with git and GitHub before joining college, there I came to know the real power of it. Version Control System was the real term and the power that I learnt there.

GSOC and ROS were the two terms that my semester full of curiosity because of which I gained an interest in web development and robotics. Apart from technical learnings, I decided to join a sport which I never played in my life thus joined the hockey club of my hostel. It was not at all easy for me to play hockey. I practised daily and even managed to enter the club's team for the interhall tournament.

Fought hard with academics as it was not easy to manage learning coding and keeping with academics and that too at the same time. Well, the curriculum of AMU is easy from other universities and, hence I was able to score decent marks.

Improvised my Python skills by reading books such as Learn Python The Hard Way. MIT's course 6.00.1(Introduction to Computer Science and Programming in Python) was a great help. Later, I made my first Pull Request with the help of AMU-OSS team.

Final Exams were over, and I decided to learn concepts of backend and thus made my plans for vacations to learn Django.
