After successful 15 days of learning computer science, Indian education system is forcing me to take a break. I have to give the end semester exams(6th semester). I shouldn't have taken a break from this if the exams will be conducted in a format other than the oral viva.

Many in this case will judge me or other like-minded people that written exams at home are easy and anyone can cheat and score good in them. But this not the case. I am whole heartedly against the oral viva because of countless reasons. Some of them are:
1. Among 100 topics if someone prepares 80 topics and he/she was asked questions from those not so prepared 20 topics. Automatically he/she will be judged as a poor candidate/student and will eventually score low.
2. A student has to waste a whole day to wait for the chance to give viva.
3. The lack of standardisation of questions can lead to situations which may be perceived as unfair. For example if a student does well in the core content then you may ask them about more advanced or different, but related, topics. Another student may do poorly in the core content but know a good deal about those extra topics for whatever reason. You’ll have to choose whether to ask standardised questions, which somewhat defeats a lot of the reasons to do an oral exam, or don’t standardise and figure out a way to limit the advantage that non-core content can add.

Anyways, Indian education was a failure and is still a failure. It's time to take the unintentional break from the real learning. Wish me luck!!
